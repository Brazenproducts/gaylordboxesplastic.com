Why Recycled PP Performs as Well as Virgin

The concern with recycled plastic packaging is always consistency — will the material behave the same way every time? For post-industrial recycled PP (PIR-PP), the answer is yes, and for good reason. Post-industrial PP comes from manufacturing offcuts and edge trim, not from mixed post-consumer waste. It's a single, clean polymer stream with known thermal history.

Elipacko's recycled PP Gaylord boxes use PIR-PP resin that is sorted, cleaned, re-pelletised, and tested for melt flow index, tensile strength, and impact resistance before entering the extrusion line. The resulting twin-wall PP sheet is mechanically equivalent to virgin PP in every specification that matters for a Gaylord box.

The only difference is a reduced carbon footprint — typically 30–40% lower embodied CO₂ versus virgin PP — and a price that is comparable or lower for high-volume orders.

Export Compliance: No Wood, No Fumigation Required

One of the most practical advantages of plastic Gaylord boxes for international shipping is ISPM 15 compliance by default. ISPM 15 is the International Standards for Phytosanitary Measures No. 15 — the global standard requiring that wood packaging used in international trade be heat-treated or fumigated to prevent the spread of invasive wood pests.

Plastic Gaylord boxes contain no wood fibre. They are exempt from ISPM 15 requirements entirely, which means no heat-treatment documentation, no fumigation delay at the port of origin, and no inspection holdups at customs. For exporters moving goods to Europe, the US, Australia, or any other country with strict phytosanitary enforcement, this alone can justify the switch from wood-reinforced cardboard Gaylords.
